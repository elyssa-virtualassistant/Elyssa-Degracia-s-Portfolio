Capacitance and Dielectrics Research
Introduction:
The capacity to store energy is rapidly becoming an indispensable component in a wide variety of systems. When compared to other types of energy storage technology, supercapacitors have a number of advantages, the most notable of which are their high power density and long cycle life.

Technical Analysis:
A capacitor is a piece of electronic equipment that can be used to store electrical charge as well as electrical energy. It is made up of at least two electrical conductors that are physically separated from one another. The capacitance C of a capacitor is defined as the ratio of the maximum charge Q that can be held in a capacitor to the voltage V that is applied across its plates.

Practical Applications:
Capacitors are commonly employed in electrical circuits because of their ability to prevent the passage of direct current (dc) while allowing alternating current (ac) to pass through.
